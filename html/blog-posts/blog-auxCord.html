    auxCord

    Posted: June 3rd, 2018
    Updated: June 15th, 2018

    Overview

    auxCord is a web application for Spotify users, originally developed during Clemson University's 2018 hackathon. The application allows two users to sync their accounts, and then generates for both users that contains a mixture of their music preferences. auxCord gets it's name from the idea's origin: when driving on a road trip with a friend, the auxiliary cord may be passed back in forth in order to share each person's preferred music. This app aims to find the music that both individuals are most likely to both enjoy and put it in one playlist for the user's convenience. auxCord won "Best Cloud Based Hack" at Clemson's 2018 Hackathon.

    Backstory

    CUhackit was Clemson's first hackathon, and one of my roommates, Angelo Carrabba , was one of the founding members. Angelo told myself and another one of our roommates, Christian Steinmetz , about the hackathon and suggested we take part. Christian was an electrical engineering major, but he had recently participated in Georgia Tech's 2017 hackathon with Angelo, and had done a lot of side projects involving software around that time. That coupled with his ridiculous work ethic and never-ending list of ideas led him to sign up for Clemson's hackathon, with the intent to win. Initially I was not one-hundred percent committed to joining, but after thinking about it a bit I also decided to join Christian in participating. One of my major motivators stemming from when Courtney , another founder of CUhackit, and Angelo told me about the first place prize being a Nintendo switch. The max team size for this hackathon was four members, so Christian and I both asked a friend ( Preston and Harold ), and thus we created our team.
